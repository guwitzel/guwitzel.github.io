Gunther Witzel

Astronomer

I am a staff scientist in the Department for Very Long Baseline (VLBI) of the Max Planck Institute for Radio Astronomy (MPIfR) in Bonn, working with Prof. A. Zensus. Before joining the MPIfR, I worked for more than six years at UCLA in the Galactic Center Group of Prof. A. Ghez, Nobel laureat of 2020. During my PhD I worked with Prof. A. Eckart at the 1. Pysikalisches Insitut of the University of Cologne. 
My main interest is focused on the central regions of galaxies, in particular of our own galaxy, the Milky Way. I am an infrared Astronomer by trade, working on observational methods to observe stars on their orbits around the supermassive black hole (SMBH) a the Galactic Center, Sagittarius A*, and to study the accretion process onto the SMBH itsself. I am working on a wide range of topics, from postprocessing methods of polarization data, precise astrometry and photometry with adaptive optics in crowded fields, to modeling of radiative processes linked to the accretion onto Sagittarius A* and statistical methods of time series analysis in general. Recently, I have extended my scope to multi-wavelegth observations including the radio and mm/submm regime, and VLBI studies. I am member of the Event Horizon Telescope, the Galactic Center Orbit Initiative, the M2FINDERS, and the Spitzer/IRAC and Chandra Sgr A* monitoring collaborations
